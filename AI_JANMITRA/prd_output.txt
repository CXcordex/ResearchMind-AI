1. Product Vision & Problem Statement
1.1 The Problem
India's 1.4 billion citizens face a three-headed crisis when interacting with the state:
Legal Complexity: New laws like BNS, BNSS, and BSA are written in dense legal language that is inaccessible to most citizens, who cannot afford a lawyer for basic guidance.
Fragmented Government Portals: Central and state scheme information is scattered across dozens of websites. Citizens lose entitlements simply because they do not know they exist or miss application deadlines.
Rising Digital Scams: Fake court summons, fraudulent utility disconnection threats, and phishing messages via WhatsApp are increasingly targeting common people with limited digital literacy.
1.2 The Vision
AI JanMitra ("People's Friend" in Hindi) is a unified, trusted, AI-powered citizen assistant that serves as a single platform combining:
Legal literacy support through plain-language law explanations
Government benefit discovery and step-by-step scheme navigation
Digital scam detection and fraud protection
Multilingual document analysis in the citizen's regional language

4. Complete User Workflow (End-to-End)
4.1 Step-by-Step Flow
USER INPUT: Citizen opens the JanMitra web app on mobile or desktop. They interact via: (a) Text chat, (b) Voice button for spoken query in regional language, or (c) Document/image upload of notice or message.
GATEWAY PROCESSING: The Node.js frontend captures the input. Voice is converted to text via Web Speech API or Whisper. Documents are uploaded to the FastAPI backend. All input types are normalized into a structured query payload.
INTENT DETECTION: The FastAPI Router Engine analyzes the query using a lightweight classification prompt. The system determines the primary intent and routes to: Nyaya-Mitra (legal issue), JanSetu (scheme enquiry), or Suraksha-AI (scam suspicion).
AGENTIC ReAct LOOP: The selected agent enters a Reason → Act → Observe → Respond cycle. It plans what information is needed, queries Supabase vector database for verified official texts, and iterates until it has enough grounded information.
REAL-TIME VERIFICATION: FastAPI background tasks verify time-sensitive information against official government APIs or public advisories (RBI, MCA, court websites, scheme portals).
RESPONSE GENERATION: The OpenRouter LLM (Meta Llama 3 or equivalent) synthesizes retrieved data into a plain-language, citizen-friendly response with clear next steps.
SAFETY GATE: The response passes through the HuggingFace moderation model. Legal disclaimers are automatically appended. Any response that could constitute definitive legal advice is softened to guidance language.
OUTPUT DELIVERY: The final response is delivered in the user's chosen regional language via text on the UI. Document drafts (RTI, complaint templates) can be downloaded. Audio playback is available via browser TTS.
5. Document Scanning & Regional Language Features
5.1 Document Authenticity Scanner (Suraksha-AI Enhancement)
Citizens often receive physical or digital documents that may be fraudulent. The Document Scanner transforms a photo into an authenticated verdict.
Supported Input Formats
JPEG/PNG photos taken with smartphone camera (most common use case)
PDF uploads (digital notices, downloaded documents)
Screenshot images (WhatsApp forwards, email screenshots)
Authenticity Check Process
Step 1 — OCR Extraction: Tesseract or HuggingFace Vision model extracts all text from the uploaded image or PDF
Step 2 — Entity Recognition: The AI identifies court names, case numbers, stamp duty amounts, official letterhead, and seals
Step 3 — Advisory Cross-Check: FastAPI queries real-time advisories from cybercrime.gov.in, RBI, and district court websites
Step 4 — Red Flag Analysis: LLM compares document against known scam patterns (urgency language, unofficial payment methods, unofficial phone numbers)
Step 5 — Verdict + Explanation: User receives AUTHENTIC / SUSPICIOUS / UNVERIFIED verdict with specific reasons highlighted
5.2 Regional Language Document Explainer (Nyaya-Mitra Enhancement)
When a user receives a legitimate but confusing legal document — property notice, RTI response, court order — the explainer makes it understandable.
Supports 8 regional languages: Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Bengali, Marathi, Gujarati, Malayalam
Explanation includes: what the document means, what action is required, by what deadline, and what happens if ignored
All explanations carry mandatory disclaimer: 'This is an AI-generated explanation to help you understand the document. Please consult a qualified lawyer for legal decisions.'
Generated explanation can be shared via WhatsApp or downloaded as PDF for offline access

8. Constraints, Risks & Mitigations
8.1 Free Tier Constraints
Supabase Free: 500 MB database, 2 GB bandwidth/month — Mitigation: Optimize embeddings, archive old queries
Render/Railway Free: Cold starts of 30–60 seconds after inactivity — Mitigation: Implement loading UI, ping service every 10 min
OpenRouter Free: Rate limits on LLM calls — Mitigation: Cache common queries, implement request queuing
HuggingFace Inference API: Free tier has throttling — Mitigation: Use lighter models, implement retry logic with exponential backoff
8.2 Content Risks & Mitigations
Hallucination Risk: LLM may generate incorrect legal information — Mitigation: Mandatory RAG from verified government texts; never answer without retrieval
Legal Liability: Users may act on AI guidance — Mitigation: Mandatory disclaimer on every response; position as 'first-step guide only'
Language Drift: Translation may lose nuance — Mitigation: Human review of sample outputs per language quarterly
Scam Database Freshness: New scam types emerge rapidly — Mitigation: Biweekly manual update of advisory vectors in Supabase
